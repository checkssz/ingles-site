Sustainability


Sustainability consists of meeting the needs of current generations without compromising the needs of future generations, while ensuring a balance between economic growth, respect for the environment, and well-being. There are several concepts related to sustainability, such as sustained growth, which is a constant and safe increase in the economy, and sustainable management, which is managing an organization valuing all the factors that encompass it, and is essentially linked to the environment.

At School


To reduce the ecological footprint
in the school, we can:

º Install selective collection bins: In selective collection, garbage is separated according to the material it is made of: plastic, glass, metal, paper, or organic materials.

º Create community gardens: Through cultivation, planting, and maintenance of the vegetable garden, the school develops in students a feeling of care and protection for nature.

º Reuse and exchange books: A great way to encourage the habit of reading, the dynamic helps students understand the importance of taking care of the material so that it can be enjoyed by other people!

º Encourage recycling and reuse of materials: The school can encourage students to give a new meaning to materials that would otherwise be discarded in nature.

º Create discussion groups on sustainability: To awaken students' autonomy, teachers can propose group debates on issues related to environmental awareness.

Reduce


Sites like this are mainly made for awareness, as that is the main way to propagate the reduce. Our idea is to share the site with the whole school and the local community, so that they can understand the effects of our ecological footprint.

Sites like the Global Footprint Network inspired our group to undertake this project. No apparent costs, we just need a computer and a good idea in our heads.

Final Thoughts


After implementing these measures, the group believes that the ecological footprint at school can be significantly reduced. By promoting waste separation, community engagement through gardening, recycling and reusing materials, fostering discussions on sustainability, conserving water resources, and adopting sustainable practices, students will develop a greater sense of environmental responsibility and contribute to a more sustainable future. It is crucial to calculate our environmental footprint using tools like the Ecological Footprint Calculator to understand the impact of our actions and further motivate the need for reduction. Together, we can make a positive difference in the world around us and create a greener and more sustainable school environment.
